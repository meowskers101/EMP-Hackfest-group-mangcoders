Saving Birds & Fish
in Washington State

Washington's rivers, wetlands, and coastlines are home to incredible wildlife — and they need our help. Here's what's at stake and what we can do.

Our Mission


We noticed that the population of some fish and birds in our community have been low and we are really passionate about animals so we decided to create this website that allows people to click and learn about saving the cute fish and birds in our beloved community. We're hoping to scale the website from just WA fish and birds to other states and maybe even nationally. We really hope that the community can use our creation and we're hoping that our love for the community and environment can help YOU become more educated on local species and maybe even love the environment even more!

Why We Want Your Help!

Migratory Shorebirds

Millions of birds travel through Washington each year along the Pacific Flyway. Coastal wetlands are their lifeline — without them, they can't survive the journey.

Pacific Salmon & Steelhead

Six salmon species call Washington home. They need cold, clean rivers to survive — but warming waters and dams are pushing them to the edge.

Coastal Marine Zones

Washington's coastline feeds seabirds and marine mammals. Ocean acidification and pollution are changing these waters faster than species can adapt.
